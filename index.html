Excessive use of plastics in weekly markets

A community survey investigating single-use plastics in weekly markets — aligned with SDG 12 — Responsible Consumption & Production.

Purpose: Present evidence from the survey, raise awareness, and recommend actionable steps for market stakeholders.

View Responses View Charts Methodology & Photos
Quick summary

41 respondents. Key issues identified: frequent use of single-use bags and packaging; low awareness of alternatives; inconsistent waste disposal at market locations. Recommended actions include reusable-bag incentives, awareness stalls, and pilot plastic collection points.

Scope

The scope of this study focuses on understanding and analyzing the extent of plastic consumption in weekly markets and evaluating its alignment with UN Sustainable Development Goal 12: Responsible Consumption and Production. This includes examining consumer behaviour, vendor practices, levels of awareness, and the availability and acceptance of sustainable alternatives to single-use plastics.

Sample size

41 responses taken in Google Form. Also, Plastic reducing awareness was also created among the local street vendors and shop owners.
